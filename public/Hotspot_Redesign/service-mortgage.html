Eligibility Requirements

Mortgage finance eligibility in Saudi Arabia typically requires the following:

  • Nationality: Saudi national (most mortgage products are available exclusively to Saudi citizens; some institutions offer options for residents)
  • Minimum Salary: SAR 5,000 per month (varies by institution and property value)
  • Employment Duration: Minimum of 3 months with current employer for government sector, 6 months for private sector
  • Credit History: Clean SIMAH record with no defaults, and a healthy debt-to-income ratio (typically below 55%)
  • Age: Between 21 and 65 years old; the financing term must end before the borrower turns 70
  • Down Payment: Minimum 10% for the first property, 30% for subsequent properties (as per SAMA regulations)
  • Property Valuation: The property must pass an independent valuation assessment conducted by a SAMA-approved appraiser

Government Support Programs

The Saudi government actively supports home ownership through several initiatives:

  • Sakani Program: Run by the Ministry of Housing and the Real Estate Development Fund (REDF), Sakani provides eligible Saudi families with subsidized mortgage profit rates, reducing the effective APR significantly. First-time homebuyers with household incomes below SAR 14,000 may qualify for a full profit subsidy.
  • REDF Support: The Real Estate Development Fund offers various financing support products including profit subsidies, down payment guarantees, and direct financing for eligible citizens.
  • Self-Build Program: If you own land and want to build your own home, you can access construction financing through participating banks with REDF support covering a portion of the profit charges.

  3. Apply and Move In: Choose your preferred offer, upload your documents (national ID, salary certificate, property deed or purchase agreement), and submit your application. Our team will guide you through the entire process until you receive your keys.

Fixed vs. Variable Profit Rates

Most mortgage products in Saudi Arabia offer a fixed profit rate for the first 2 to 5 years, after which the rate may become variable and adjust based on the Saudi Interbank Offered Rate (SAIBOR). When comparing offers on Hotspot, pay close attention to whether the rate is fixed for the entire term or only for an initial period. A fully fixed rate provides payment certainty, while a variable rate may start lower but could increase over time. Hotspot clearly labels each offer so you can make an informed decision.

Sakani Eligibility Tip

To maximize your government support, register on the Sakani platform before applying for a mortgage. Ensure your household information is up to date and that you have not previously received government housing support. Sakani benefits can significantly reduce your monthly payments, sometimes by as much as 50% of the profit charges, making home ownership far more affordable.
