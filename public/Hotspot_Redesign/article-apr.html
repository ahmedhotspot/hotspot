What is APR and Why Does it Matter?

5 min read March 15, 2026
Back to Financial Guides

What is Annual Percentage Rate (APR)?

The Annual Percentage Rate (APR) is the total annual cost of financing expressed as a percentage. This rate includes not only the base interest rate, but also all fees and costs associated with the financing.

APR is the most accurate measure for comparing different financing offers because it reflects the true total cost.

Why Does APR Matter?

When comparing financing offers from different banks, you may find that the advertised interest rates are similar. However, the actual cost can vary significantly due to additional fees. APR helps you:

  • Compare offers fairly and accurately across different banks
  • Understand the true cost of financing including all fees
  • Make an informed decision that saves you money in the long run
  • Avoid offers that look attractive on the surface but are actually expensive

How is APR Calculated?

APR is calculated by taking all financing costs into account, including:

  • Base Interest Rate: The percentage applied to the borrowed amount
  • Processing Fees: Administrative charges for handling the financing application
  • Valuation Fees: Costs for appraising collateral (in the case of mortgages)
  • Insurance Fees: Costs of mandatory insurance linked to the financing

Practical Tips for Comparison

When searching for the best financing offer, follow these tips:

  • Always ask for the APR, not just the interest rate
  • Compare at least 3 offers from different banks
  • Use Hotspot's financing calculator to easily compare offers
  • Make sure the repayment period is the same when comparing offers

According to SAMA (Saudi Central Bank) regulations, all banks and financial institutions must clearly disclose the APR in all financing offers.
