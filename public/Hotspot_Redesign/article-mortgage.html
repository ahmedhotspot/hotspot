First-Time Homebuyer Requirements in KSA

7 min read March 10, 2026
Back to Financial Guides

Ready to Buy Your First Home?

Buying your first home is one of the most important financial decisions in your life. In Saudi Arabia, there are several government programs and initiatives that support citizens in owning their first home, most notably the Sakani program and the Real Estate Development Fund (REDF).

First-time buyers can benefit from the real estate transaction tax exemption for the first home up to SAR 1,000,000.

Basic Eligibility Criteria

  • Nationality: Applicant must be a Saudi citizen
  • Age: Minimum 20 years old, maximum 70 years at the end of the financing term
  • Monthly Income: Minimum varies by bank (typically SAR 3,500 to 5,000)
  • Employment Duration: Minimum 1 month in government sector or 3 months in private sector
  • Credit History: No previous defaults on record
  • Debt-to-Income Ratio: Must not exceed 65% of salary as per SAMA regulations

Required Documents

Document Details
National ID Valid copy of Saudi National ID
Salary Certificate Recent letter from employer (not older than 60 days)
Bank Statements Last 3 to 6 months
SIMAH Report Recent credit report
Property Title Deed Copy of the title deed or sale contract
Building Permit Required for self-build applications

Application Steps

  1. Check your eligibility through the Sakani platform or Real Estate Development Fund
  2. Compare mortgage offers from different banks through Hotspot
  3. Choose a suitable property and ensure it meets the requirements
  4. Submit the financing application with all required documents
  5. Wait for approval and completion of the property valuation
  6. Sign the financing contract and receive your new home

Government Support Programs

The Saudi government offers several programs to support first-time homeownership:

  • Sakani Program: Provides various options including ready units, land plots, and self-build
  • Real Estate Development Fund: Offers subsidized financing up to SAR 500,000
  • Tax Exemption: Exemption from real estate transaction tax for the first home

We recommend applying to the Sakani program first to check your eligibility for government support before applying for bank financing.
