How to Improve Your Credit Score

6 min read March 5, 2026
Back to Financial Guides

What is a Credit Score?

A credit score is a numerical rating that reflects your creditworthiness based on your financial history. In Saudi Arabia, the Saudi Credit Bureau (SIMAH) issues credit reports that banks rely on to make financing decisions.

Understanding Credit Score Ranges

700+ Excellent
650 - 699 Good
600 - 649 Fair
Below 600 Poor

The higher your credit score, the better your chances of getting financing with lower interest rates and more flexible terms.

You can check your credit report for free once a year through the official SIMAH website.

7 Steps to Improve Your Credit Score

1

Pay Your Bills on Time

Even a single day of late payment negatively affects your report. Set up automatic transfers to ensure timely payments.

2

Reduce Credit Card Utilization

Try not to exceed 30% of your available credit limit. Lower utilization = higher score.

3

Don't Apply for Multiple Loans at Once

Each financing application is recorded as an inquiry on your report. Multiple inquiries in a short period give a negative impression.

4

Keep Old Accounts Open

The length of your credit history matters. Don't close old credit cards even if you don't use them frequently.

5

Diversify Your Credit Sources

Having a mix of different credit types (credit card, auto finance, etc.) improves your score.

6

Review Your Report Regularly

Check your SIMAH report to ensure there are no errors or inaccurate information, and file a dispute immediately if you find any.

7

Pay Off Existing Debts

Start by paying off the highest-interest debts first. Reducing your total debt significantly boosts your score.

How Long Does It Take to Improve?

Improving your credit score isn't instant. It typically takes 3 to 6 months of consistent good practices to see noticeable improvement. In some cases (such as major defaults), it may take a year or more.

Don't wait until you need financing to start improving your score. Start now and you'll be ready when you need to apply.
