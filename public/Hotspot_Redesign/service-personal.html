Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for personal finance in Saudi Arabia, you generally need to meet the following criteria. Keep in mind that specific requirements may vary between banks and financial institutions:

  • Nationality: Saudi national or legal resident with a valid Iqama
  • Minimum Salary: SAR 3,500 per month (some banks may require higher depending on the loan amount)
  • Employment Duration: Minimum of 1 month with your current employer (government sector) or 3 months (private sector)
  • Credit History: Clean credit record with no defaults or delinquent payments reported to SIMAH
  • Age: Between 21 and 60 years old at the time of application
  • Salary Transfer: Salary must be transferred to the lending bank (required by most institutions)

Understanding APR

When comparing personal finance offers, always look at the Annual Percentage Rate (APR) rather than just the flat rate. The APR includes all fees and charges, giving you a true picture of the total cost of borrowing. Hotspot displays the APR for every offer so you can make an informed comparison. A lower APR means less total cost over the life of your financing, even if the monthly installment appears similar to a higher-APR offer.

Tips for Getting the Best Rate

To improve your chances of getting approved with the best possible rate, make sure your SIMAH credit report is clean, keep your debt-to-income ratio below 45%, and consider opting for salary transfer to the lending bank. Banks typically offer lower rates to customers who transfer their salary, as it reduces risk and ensures timely repayment.
