Types of Credit Cards

Different credit cards cater to different needs and spending habits. Here are the main categories available in Saudi Arabia:

  • Cashback Cards: Ideal for everyday spenders who want to earn money back on regular purchases. These cards typically offer between 1% and 5% cashback on categories like groceries, fuel, dining, and bills. The cashback is usually credited to your statement monthly.
  • Travel & Miles Cards: Designed for frequent travelers, these cards earn airline miles or travel points on every purchase. Benefits often include airport lounge access, priority boarding, complimentary travel insurance, and hotel loyalty program partnerships.
  • Rewards Cards: These versatile cards earn points on all purchases that can be redeemed for a wide range of items including electronics, gift vouchers, airline tickets, and charitable donations. Some programs allow point transfers to airline loyalty programs.
  • Premium & Signature Cards: High-end cards for affluent customers offering premium benefits such as dedicated concierge services, unlimited lounge access worldwide, higher reward earning rates, complimentary hotel nights, and exclusive event invitations.
  • Islamic Credit Cards: Shariah-compliant credit cards that operate without interest charges. Instead, a fixed fee or profit margin is applied to outstanding balances. These cards offer the same rewards and benefits as conventional cards while adhering to Islamic finance principles.

Eligibility Requirements

General eligibility criteria for credit cards in Saudi Arabia include:

  • Nationality: Saudi national or legal resident with a valid Iqama
  • Minimum Salary: SAR 3,000 per month for basic cards; SAR 10,000 to SAR 30,000+ for premium and signature cards
  • Employment: Must be currently employed with a minimum of 1 month at current employer, or self-employed with documented income
  • Credit History: Clean SIMAH record; some premium cards require an existing banking relationship
  • Age: Minimum 18 years old (21 for some premium cards)

How to Choose the Right Card

Selecting the best credit card depends on your spending habits and financial goals:

  1. Analyze Your Spending: Review where most of your money goes each month. If you spend heavily on groceries and fuel, a cashback card will save you the most. If you travel frequently, a miles card offers greater value.
  2. Compare Annual Fees vs. Benefits: Premium cards charge higher annual fees but offer more generous rewards. Calculate whether the value of the rewards and benefits exceeds the annual fee based on your expected spending.
  3. Check the Profit Rate: If you plan to carry a balance, the profit rate on outstanding balances matters significantly. Compare the monthly profit rate across cards and aim for the lowest rate available.

Credit Card Best Practices

To get the most from your credit card while staying financially healthy, always pay your full balance before the due date to avoid profit charges. Set up automatic payments to ensure you never miss a due date, as late payments negatively affect your SIMAH credit score. Keep your credit utilization below 30% of your total credit limit. If you have multiple cards, focus your spending on the card that offers the best rewards for each category and review your statements monthly for any unauthorized transactions.
