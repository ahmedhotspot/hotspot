Eligibility Requirements

Auto finance eligibility varies between institutions, but the general requirements include:

  • Nationality: Saudi national or legal resident with a valid Iqama (minimum 3 months remaining)
  • Minimum Salary: SAR 3,500 per month for basic vehicles; higher salaries may be required for luxury or high-value vehicles
  • Employment Duration: Minimum of 1 month for government employees, or 3 months for private sector employees
  • Credit History: Clean SIMAH credit record with no active defaults or bounced checks
  • Age: Between 21 and 65 years old at the time of application
  • Down Payment: Minimum 10% for new cars and 15% for used cars (varies by institution)
  • Vehicle Age: Used vehicles must typically be no older than 5 years at the time of purchase

Lease vs. Purchase: Which Is Right for You?

In Saudi Arabia, you can either lease a vehicle (Ijarah) or purchase it outright through a financing agreement (Murabaha). Each option has its advantages:

  • Leasing (Ijarah): Lower monthly payments, option to upgrade your car at the end of the lease, maintenance may be included, and you do not own the vehicle until the end of the contract.
  • Purchase (Murabaha): You own the vehicle from day one (registered in your name), you can sell or modify the car freely, and total cost may be lower over the full term compared to leasing.

Important: Total Cost of Ownership

When comparing auto finance offers, consider the total cost of ownership beyond just the monthly installment. Factor in comprehensive insurance premiums, annual registration fees, maintenance costs, fuel expenses, and depreciation. Hotspot displays the total financing cost for each offer so you can see exactly how much you will pay over the life of the contract. Remember, a longer repayment period means lower monthly payments but a higher total cost.
